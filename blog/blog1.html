What will happen to Bing Chun


Posted on April 12, 2026

In Sri Lanka, Bing Chun changed the bubble tea market by lowering prices to around Rs. 300. This move made bubble tea available to a lot more people at a time when it was seen as a high-end product. It quickly created huge demand. We even saw long lines that lasted more than an hour at the beginning, which showed how much people liked the idea of "affordable expensive products." Because of that, the brand expanded quickly and opened many branches, and obviously they seem to be making good profit from the volume. but now the lines are gone and the company hopes to make a lot of money. They said they would open many branches frequently, They sold bubble tea cheaply, and they lowered the quality of the product. but the main problem is quality. People have a plus mind because they offer bubble tea cheaply, but people also have a minus mind because the quality is very low (because they use a lot of water instead of milk, which lowers the quality). But the real question is, how long can this go on? People might keep buying it because it's cheap and easy to find, but low quality products don't usually make people loyal for a long time. Most people will eat it a lot just because it's cheap, but when it comes to something they really like or value, they'll still choose a better quality option, even if it's less often.
